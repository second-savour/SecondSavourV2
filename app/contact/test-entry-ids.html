Follow these steps to find your entry IDs:

Step 1: Open your Google Form in a new tab:
Click here to open your form
Step 2: Right-click anywhere on the form and select Inspect (or press F12)
Step 3: Press Ctrl+F (or Cmd+F on Mac) in the Developer Tools
Step 4: Search for: entry.
Step 5: Look for input fields with names like entry.1234567890

What to look for:

Find these fields in the HTML:

  • Name field: <input name="entry.XXXXXXXX">
  • Email field: <input name="entry.YYYYYYYY">
  • Type of Question: <select name="entry.ZZZZZZZZ"> or radio buttons
  • If Other: <input name="entry.AAAAAAAA">
  • Questions/Comments: <textarea name="entry.BBBBBBBB">

Alternative Method - Use Pre-fill URL:

Step 1: Go to your Google Form (as form owner)
Step 2: Click the three dots menu (‚ãÆ) and select "Get pre-filled link"
Step 3: Fill in dummy data in each field
Step 4: Click "Get link" - the URL will show all entry IDs
Example URL:
https://docs.google.com/forms/.../viewform?entry.123=Name&entry.456=email@test.com

Once you have the entry IDs:

Update them in /app/contact/page.js in the ENTRY_IDS object:

const ENTRY_IDS = {
  name: "entry.XXXXXXXX",
  email: "entry.YYYYYYYY",
  questionType: "entry.ZZZZZZZZ",

};
